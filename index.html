Statistical Analysis on Factors Influencing Life Expectancy

Due to the advancement of technology and medicine, the global life expectancy has increased by more than 6 years between 2000 and 2015. However, there are also other factors related to the increase in life expectancy that vary between different countries. Many of these factors are related to the economic status of a country, while some of the other factors are related to government funding towards healthcare and the state of public health.


The world average life expectancy in 1950 was 48 years, compared to the current world average of 71.8 years in 2015. As shown in the map, most countries have an average life expectancy of 60+ years.


Economy

As can be seen in the graph on the left, there seems to be a correlation between a country's economic status and its average life expectancy. The more developed the country is, the more capable it is to provide healthcare and infrastructure for the people.
